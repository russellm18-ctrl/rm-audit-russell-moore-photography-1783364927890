// Findings

  • THE STRUCTURAL METADATA COLLAPSE: Your commercial page uses service category labels like "Automotive Performance Capture" and "La Marzocco Heritage & Craft" as display text, but without JSON-LD ImageObject or Service schema binding those labels to actual crawlable structured data, Google reads your portfolio as a decorative image wall, not a commercial service provider. You are bleeding the query "commercial product photographer Olympia WA" to competitors whose sites have even basic LocalBusiness schema deployed, despite you explicitly claiming schema instrumentation as a core deliverable. A photographer selling structured data implementation who doesn't have it visibly firing on his own commercial page is a credibility liability, not a proof point.
  • THE HIDDEN TRUST DEFICIT: An AEC project manager or athletic director with a $15,000+ budget authorization will land on your commercial page, read "High-Yield Infrastructure," and find zero before/after performance comparisons, zero documented search lift from a completed build, and zero client-facing case study with named outcomes. You name South Puget Sound Habitat for Humanity twice and cite 821 delivered assets across 8 shoot days, but there is no visual or data evidence showing what those assets actually did for their web presence, donor engagement, or search visibility. Executives buying at that price floor are buying proof of outcome, not proof of volume.
  • THE COMPETITIVE EXPOSURE: A sharp Pacific Northwest commercial photographer with AEC positioning—say, operating out of Seattle or Tacoma with established contractor relationships—is almost certainly presenting completed project walkthroughs: a before-construction-to-occupancy visual arc with geo-tagged assets, embedded in client websites and ranking for project-specific queries. You have "Habitat Build Community Impact" listed as a portfolio category with no published project URL, no embedded map data, no schema-linked location tying it to South Puget Sound search geography—meaning a general contractor in Thurston County searching for documented AEC visual work finds your competitor's indexed project page before your uncrawled portfolio image.
  • THE PHOTO-FIRST UPGRADE: Your three-phase Infrastructure Build framework—Schematic, Construct, Telemetry—is a legitimate differentiator, but it is currently described as a process rather than demonstrated as a working system on your own domain. Deploy one fully instrumented public case study using the Habitat for Humanity engagement: publish a dedicated project page with JSON-LD JobPosting or LocalBusiness cross-reference, GA4 event annotations showing asset delivery milestones, and geotagged ImageObject schema tied to specific Thurston County project addresses. That single page becomes a live sales tool that proves your methodology to every $15K+ buyer before they schedule a Schematic call.
